## 1. Executive Summary

The S&P 500 enters March 2026 at **6,878.88** — essentially flat year-to-date (+0.30%) after a strong 12-month run (+17.59%). Beneath the surface calm, a significant rotation is underway: cyclical and defensive sectors (Energy, Industrials, Materials, Utilities, Consumer Staples) are outperforming sharply, while Technology and Financials — which drove 2025's gains — are now lagging.

The macro backdrop is mixed. Inflation is near the Fed's target (2.39% YoY CPI), the Fed is still cutting rates (3.64%), and unemployment ticked down to 4.3%. However, GDP growth decelerated sharply to 1.4% annualized in Q4 2025 from 4.4% in Q3, and consumer sentiment (UMich 56.4) remains well below historical norms (~85), signaling household stress.

Investor sentiment is **fearful** (CNN Fear & Greed: 42.9/100). Major geopolitical risk has emerged with U.S.-Israeli strikes on Iran and reports of Khamenei's death. Warren Buffett's retirement accompanied by a record cash position ($187–373B at Berkshire Hathaway) has sent a widely-noted caution signal to markets.

**Bottom line:** The market is in a late-cycle rotation with elevated geopolitical risk, decelerating growth, and pockets of valuation excess in mega-cap tech. Selective repositioning toward defensives and real assets is warranted. Investors should hold core equity exposure but tilt toward outperforming sectors and maintain cash optionality for a potential 5–10% correction.
